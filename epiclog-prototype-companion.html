Design Principles

1Friendly, not flashy. Looks like a journal you've kept for years — paper warmth, soft edges, hand-written notes.
2Place & texture first. Photos, stamps, dashed borders, and earth tones evoke real travel — not a CRM.
3Helpful, never pushy. The companion suggests gently. Capture is one tap, but nothing demands attention.
4Calm progress. No confetti explosions — just a quiet stamp when you complete something special.

Cedar for the path. Terracotta for sun-bleached rooftops. Saffron for spice and late afternoons. Twilight indigo for the long walk home. The palette is borrowed from places, not screens.

How it feels different

★Soft shadows, dashed circles, paper grain — surfaces feel handled, not rendered.
★Lora italic + Caveat handwriting — every screen has a personal voice tucked into it.
★Itinerary timeline view — places connect with a dashed line, like a planned walk.
★Passport-stamp achievements — circular dashed badges instead of trophy icons.
★Photo-led cards — gradient placeholders read as real travel imagery.
★Companion micro-copy — "a thought from your companion", "another one for the journal" — small kindnesses everywhere.

· Tap a list card → open the journey
· Drag the grabber to expand to 92%

· Swipe a list card right → stamp celebration
